About the Principal

Clear, common-sense counsel for nations under pressure.

John deVerteuil brings a profound and diverse military background in national security policy to the work of nation building. As an Independent Consultant and book author, his focus is straightforward: clear, common-sense solutions that hold up under the conditions where they actually have to operate.

The Advisory Team is built to the demands of each national issue — Security, Rule of Law, Economic, and Infrastructure Development. The world stage has been in turmoil for decades, and failed or developing nations require a team of experts to assess, develop, and execute a sustainable national strategy. Nation Building targets not just the security and economic demands of the nation, but the local population and the advancement of economic stability at the community level.

The United States 2025 National Strategy identifies the demand for regional security through economic means. Executed correctly, that approach delivers long-term economic growth, raises standards of living, and produces a durably secure region. Latin American populations are electing leaders who support precisely this kind of properly planned and executed strategy. Nation Building Advisory Group stands with those leaders, to assist in every aspect of their future.

Practice Areas

Four pillars, one coherent strategy.

i.
Security
Assessing and strengthening national security posture — military, police, intelligence, and border capacity — so that gains in every other domain have a stable foundation to rest on.
ii.
Rule of Law
Building credible, functioning legal and judicial institutions: courts, prosecutorial bodies, anti-corruption mechanisms, and the everyday processes that determine whether citizens trust the state.
iii.
Economic Development
Advancing economic stability at both national and local levels — investment climate, fiscal discipline, and the community-scale economic activity that raises standards of living where it is felt.
iv.
Infrastructure Development
Sequencing the physical and institutional infrastructure — energy, transport, water, communications — that underwrites long-term growth and a durably secure region.
v.
National Strategy & Execution
The work that ties it together: assessing conditions on the ground, developing a sustainable national strategy across all four pillars, and standing with leadership through execution.
